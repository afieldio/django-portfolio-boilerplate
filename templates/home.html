wings to websites

Hello! My name is Adam.

I am a Technical project manager who has delivered award winning products for Google and Saatchi & Saatchi. An advocate of agile thinking and lover of efficiency and process. I also like to dabble in code, have a life long ambition to become a polymath and think Aquaponics will help solve the food crisis.

Question is, how did I get here?

Adam the pilot

I started my career as a commercial pilot and have over 3000 hours on a Boeing 737-800. I learnt a lot about effective communication, leadership qualities and working as part of a team. I also learnt that whilst the devil is in the detail you can never afford to lose sight of the bigger picture.

After nearly seven years of flying my contract came to an end I was at one of those forks in life. I decided to take the red pill and haven't looked back!

Adam the student

After leaving aviation I went back to University to study for a BSc in web development where I gained a first class degree. It was an intensive two years and where we covered UX and HCI as well as gaining lots of hands on experience with multiple languages including JAVA, JS and PHP.

My final dissertation focused on creating a closed loop Aquaponic system that was monitored and controlled using a Raspberry Pi and Arduino. Since leaving university I have continued to work on this project and built a larger system in my garden. It might possibly be the UK's first IoT Aquaponic shed powered by Henry and his outlaws (the goldfish!).

Adam the project manager

I have been working at Potato for nearly three years and have had the pleasure of working on a wide range of projects. Working mainly for Google most of the projects are under NDA's. I can say that my projects have ranged from complex bits of software and CMS's as well as managing the ongoing maintenance of large site that receives millions of hits a day.

PMing at Potato is not quite like other Agencies. I cover all phases of the project life cycle from stakeholder consultation and requirements gathering through to managing an agile development process with teams of UX/Design and Developers.

Award winning projects

These awards wouldn't have been won without the hard work from lots of people involved. Nice to see something you have worked on get some recognition though!

Virtual Art Sessions

We were part of a team working on a Chrome Experiment that displayed a range digital artwork and illustrations. Using Google's Tilt Brush the artists created 3D designs, the results of which are quite spectacular. This site was turned around in a matter of weeks.

FWA of the Day - 9 May 2016

The Vocal Adventure

Working in collaboration with Saatchi & Saatchi this was incredible project to be involved with! The aim was to highlight issues faced by people who rely on text to voice synthesizers. Using the Chrome's HTML5 speech recognition API, users navigate the site by narrating sections of a story.

FWA of the Day - 13 May 2016

FWA Cutting edge award - 11 Jul 2016
